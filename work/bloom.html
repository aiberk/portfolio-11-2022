Bloom Market: Savannah Food Desserts

54.4 million Americans live in food desserts, lacking meaningful access to fresh and healthy food close to their homes and neighborhoods. Bloom Market provides easy and accessible healthy nutritious food via a customer facing mobile app and an autonomous delivery and pop-up shop food truck. In a ten week sprint, for a Human Factors class at Savannah College of Art and Design, I was asked to create a product that would help people in the neighboring community of West Savannah.



My role consisted of three parts: management, research and design. During the research phase I consulted with domain experts, documentation, and target consumers. As well as tested resonance of ideas and tangible prototypes. During the design phase I was in charge of designing the final vehicle, designing map and other feature on the app, and running resonance tests on final solutions. I was also in charge of keeping the group on track and aligning closely with our sprint leader, coordinating people and actionable decisions.



The research phase of this project started by observing problems that affect Americans in their everyday lives. Based on the team’s popular vote and the fact that is it is a local issue we decided to base our project on food desserts. We conducted 6 interviews with stakeholders and field experts collecting a total of 334 Minutes of Interview time. As well as surveying 224 people with an age range from 23 to 51.



After completing our research phase, we created user journeys and personas to guide us in making our final product.
