Flyer: The doing good app

Flyer is a platform that connects community members to local non-profit organizations in a way that is personalized to the interests and needs of both parties. Regular users of Flyer can indicate what causes they are interested in and how they’d like to help out their community. Non-profits are able to be discovered based on what cause(s) they are tagged with and source different local volunteers based on their needs.

This project was developed in collaboration with 3 other students as a Senior Thesis project at Savannah College of Art and Design. We were asked to find a problem of our choice and design a solution. My role for this project mostly based as a researcher, and interface designer. I was in charge of designing a research plan and executing interviews with users and analyzing results. This work help create our personas and user journeys. Lastly I took part in designing and testing the final interfaces for mobile, desktop, and a physical product based on user stories created by the PM. The problem we decided to tackle was that there isn't a streamlined and focused way for community members to turn digital engagements into real-world actionable good. Our solution involved designing a convenient platform for both volunteers and organizers to make a positive and tangible impact on the world. With a modern and robust interface streamlined with the necessary tools to achieve goals in their charitable pursuit.
